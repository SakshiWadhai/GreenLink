Waste Management Research

Technologies Initially Introduced in Waste Management

Traditional methods like landfilling and incineration caused environmental issues. Later, technologies like mechanized waste segregation, composting, and waste-to-energy (WTE) plants were introduced. However, challenges like high costs and infrastructure limitations restricted widespread adoption.

Why Some Technologies Are in Use and Others Are Not

Cost-effective and eco-friendly solutions like biogas production and plastic recycling thrive, while high-cost and emission-heavy technologies struggle due to lack of infrastructure and regulatory support.

Government-Initiated Projects in Waste Management

Programs like India’s Swachh Bharat Mission and the EU Circular Economy Action Plan focus on waste reduction and recycling. Success depends on public participation and proper waste segregation.

Individuals Who Have Contributed to Waste Reduction

Afroz Shah led the world’s largest beach cleanup, and Boyan Slat pioneered ocean plastic removal technology. These efforts show how individuals can drive large-scale environmental change.
